2  Use of the Microscope

2.1 Objectives

In this laboratory session you will learn to use an essential tool in the biologist’s kit, the compound light microscope. Carefully read and closely follow the following instructions before you begin this activity in order to avoid damaging the microscope in your care.

2.2 Your objectives for this lab are:

  • Identify the parts of the microscope and list the function of each.
  • Demonstrate the proper techniques for use, care, and transportation of the microscope.
  • Define and demonstrate a working understanding of the concepts of total magnification, resolution, parfocal, field diameter, depth of field, and working distance.
  • Apply microscopy skills to properly focus, view, and manipulate a variety of histology slides.
  • Explain why the microscope’s field of view decreases as you increase the magnification.

2.3 Terms to Learn

  • Iris diaphragm
  • Condenser adjustment knob

2.4 General Rules

  • Always carry the microscope using two hands: one holding the arm and the other supporting the base.
  • Do not touch the objective lenses. Keep the scanning lens in position when storing.
  • Clean lenses only with lens paper and lens cleaning solution.
  • Report malfunctions to your instructor; do not attempt repairs.

2.5 Returning the Microscope

  • Lower the stage
  • Rotate to scanning (4x) lens
  • Remove and clean slide
  • Wrap cord properly
  • Replace dust cover
  • Carry with two hands

2.8 Magnification

Student microscopes typically have four objective lenses:

  • Scanning (4x)
  • Low power (10x)
  • High power (40x)
  • Oil immersion (100x)

The ocular lens is typically 10x.

2.10 Focusing on Specimens

Follow the three-step focusing process:

2.10.1 1. Scanning Lens (4x)

  • Start with stage lowered
  • Turn on light
  • Place specimen
  • Use coarse focus, then fine focus
  • Center specimen

2.10.2 2. Low Power (10x)

  • Rotate turret
  • Use fine focus only
  • Re-center specimen

2.10.3 3. High Power (40x)

  • Rotate to 40x
  • Use fine focus only
  • Adjust diaphragm and light as needed

2.12 Focusing Exercises

2.12.1 Letter “e” Observation

  • The image appears upside down and reversed compared to the original.

2.12.2 Depth of Field (Threads)

  • More adjustment is needed at higher magnification.
  • At high power, not all layers are in focus simultaneously.
  • This shows that depth of field decreases as magnification increases.

2.13 Examining Tissues

Observe prepared slides at:

  • 40x
  • 100x
  • 400x

2.14 End of Lab

  • Clean and return slides
  • Turn off microscope
  • Wrap cord
  • Set scanning lens
  • Lower stage
  • Cover microscope
  • Return to cabinet

2.15 Troubleshooting

Image too dark? - Check power, light, rheostat, diaphragm

Cannot see clearly? - Clean lenses
- Check slide orientation
- Adjust focus

Half-moon image? - Objective not clicked in

Headache? - Adjust ocular distance and focus
- Take breaks
